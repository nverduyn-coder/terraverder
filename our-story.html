Our story

Made with purpose,
worn with pride.

TerraVerde began with a linen tablecloth, a grandmother's kitchen, and a question: why does beautiful design have to harm the world that inspires it?

What we stand for

Three things we never compromise on

From our first placemat to our thousandth order, these three principles guide every decision we make — about materials, production, pricing and partnerships.

01

Honest materials

If we can't trace it to its source and vouch for it, it doesn't go into our products. No exceptions, no compromises, no greenwashing.

02

People first

The artisans who make our products deserve more than a fair wage — they deserve respect, stability and pride in what they create. We pay 35% above minimum.

03

Beauty that lasts

We design for decades, not seasons. A TerraVerde placemat should still be on your table in 2040. If it wears out before then, we'll fix it.

Emma de VriesFounder & Creative Director, TerraVerde
The founder

From a linen tablecloth to a brand built on conviction

I grew up watching my grandmother lay the table every Sunday with the same set of linen placemats she'd had since the 1970s. They were soft, faded in all the right places, and more beautiful for every wash. That table taught me that the best things don't wear out — they wear in.

In 2021, after years working in sustainable fashion, I looked at the homeware market and saw the same problem I'd seen in clothing: beautiful design and genuine responsibility were treated as opposites. Either you bought fast, cheap, synthetic, or you paid a premium for something that claimed to be sustainable but couldn't prove it.

"I wanted to make something my grandmother would have bought — and kept for fifty years."

TerraVerde launched in 2022 with twelve products, a waiting list and a very clear commitment: every claim we make about sustainability will be independently certified, every person who makes our products will be fairly paid, and every product we sell will be worth keeping forever.

We're still small. We still visit every workshop ourselves. And we still lay our own tables with TerraVerde every morning.

Emma de Vries
How we got here

The TerraVerde journey

2021

The idea takes root

Emma de Vries begins sourcing sustainable linen from Belgian mills and visiting artisan workshops in Jaipur. The concept for TerraVerde is written on a napkin.

2022

Launch with 12 products

TerraVerde goes live with a range of linen placemats, napkins and one jute bag. The first 200 orders sell out in 3 weeks. The waiting list begins.

2023

GOTS and OEKO-TEX certification

After 18 months of working towards it, TerraVerde receives GOTS certification across all cotton and linen products, and OEKO-TEX® Standard 100 for dyes.

2024

The take-back programme launches

We introduce our circular take-back service — the first of its kind in the Dutch sustainable homeware market. 140 products returned and upcycled in year one.

2025

2,847 homes and counting

We pass 2,000 customers, add Portuguese linen artisans as production partners, and eliminate the last traces of plastic from our packaging and supply chain.

2026

Next chapter

New collections, B2B partnerships with conscious restaurants and hotels, and a seasonal subscription box launching later this year. The table keeps getting longer.

The people behind every product

Our artisan partners

We work with 14 family workshops across India and Portugal. These are not anonymous factories — they are people we know by name, visit in person and are proud to call partners.

The Sharma Workshop

📍 Jaipur, India · Est. 1978

Three generations of textile weavers. Ravi Sharma and his daughters make all of our linen placemats and napkins. They've been weaving together since Ravi was eight years old.

Casa Textil Alentejo

📍 Évora, Portugal · Est. 1991

Maria and her team of twelve weavers specialise in heavy stonewashed linen. Their workshop in Évora has been supplying European home brands for over 30 years.

The Gupta Jute Collective

📍 Kolkata, India · Est. 2003

A women-led cooperative of 22 artisans who hand-weave all of our jute and seagrass products. TerraVerde is their largest and longest-standing partner.
